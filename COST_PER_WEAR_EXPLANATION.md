# Cost Per Wear (CPW) Calculation in ClosetIQ

## What is Cost Per Wear?

Cost Per Wear (CPW) is a metric that helps you understand the true value of your clothing purchases by calculating how much each wear of an item costs you.

## Basic Formula

```
Cost Per Wear = Purchase Price ÷ Number of Times Worn
```

## Examples

### Example 1: Basic Calculation
- **Item**: Blue jeans
- **Purchase Price**: ₹2,000
- **Times Worn**: 20
- **CPW**: ₹2,000 ÷ 20 = ₹100 per wear

### Example 2: Expensive Item
- **Item**: Designer saree
- **Purchase Price**: ₹15,000
- **Times Worn**: 3
- **CPW**: ₹15,000 ÷ 3 = ₹5,000 per wear

## Smart CPW Calculation in ClosetIQ

ClosetIQ uses an enhanced CPW calculation that considers:

### 1. Price-Based Expectations
Different price ranges have different expected wear counts:

- **₹20,000+**: Expected 4 wears (ultra-luxury/designer pieces)
- **₹10,000-₹19,999**: Expected 8 wears (luxury/wedding wear)
- **₹5,000-₹9,999**: Expected 15 wears (formal/occasion wear)
- **₹2,000-₹4,999**: Expected 25 wears (work/smart casual)
- **Under ₹2,000**: Expected 40 wears (everyday/casual)

### 2. Category-Specific Logic
Traditional and formal wear have different expectations:

- **Sarees/Lehengas (₹15,000+)**: Expected 3 wears
- **Sarees/Lehengas (₹8,000-₹14,999)**: Expected 6 wears
- **Sarees/Lehengas (₹3,000-₹7,999)**: Expected 12 wears
- **Evening Gowns**: Similar to traditional wear expectations

### 3. Current vs Target CPW
When an item hasn't reached its expected wear count, ClosetIQ shows:

```
Current: ₹5,000 | Target: ₹1,000 (15 wears)
```

This means:
- You've currently achieved ₹5,000 per wear
- If you wear it 15 times total, you'll achieve ₹1,000 per wear

## How CPW Updates in ClosetIQ

1. **When you add an item**: CPW = Full purchase price (0 wears)
2. **When you log an outfit**: 
   - Each selected item's wear count increases by 1
   - CPW automatically recalculates: Price ÷ New Wear Count

## Using CPW for Smart Shopping

### Good CPW Indicators:
- **Daily wear items**: Under ₹100 per wear
- **Work clothes**: Under ₹200 per wear
- **Occasion wear**: Under ₹2,000 per wear
- **Traditional/formal**: Under ₹3,000 per wear

### Red Flags:
- **High CPW after many wears**: Item might not suit your lifestyle
- **Never worn items**: CPW equals full purchase price
- **Rarely worn expensive items**: Poor value for money

## CPW in Purchase Decisions

When analyzing a potential purchase, ClosetIQ considers:

1. **Initial CPW**: What will the first wear cost?
2. **Target CPW**: What will it cost after expected wears?
3. **Similar items**: Do you already own similar pieces with high CPW?

### Example Purchase Analysis:
**Considering**: Navy blazer for ₹6,000

**Analysis**:
- Initial CPW: ₹6,000 (first wear)
- Target CPW: ₹400 (after 15 expected wears)
- **Recommendation**: BUY if you don't have similar blazers, DON'T BUY if you already own 3+ similar blazers with high CPW

## Tips for Better CPW

1. **Wear new items quickly**: Don't let items sit unworn
2. **Mix and match**: Create multiple outfits with each piece
3. **Declutter high-CPW items**: Remove items you rarely wear
4. **Buy versatile pieces**: Items that work for multiple occasions
5. **Consider cost per wear before buying**: Will you realistically wear this enough?

## CPW Tracking Benefits

- **Identifies underused items**: High CPW items need more wear or decluttering
- **Validates good purchases**: Low CPW items prove their worth
- **Guides future shopping**: Avoid buying similar items to high-CPW pieces
- **Encourages outfit variety**: Rotate through your wardrobe more effectively
- **Builds sustainable habits**: Buy less, wear more, waste less

Remember: The goal isn't to have the lowest CPW possible, but to make conscious decisions about your clothing investments and ensure you're getting good value from your wardrobe!
